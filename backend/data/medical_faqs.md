# Medical FAQs for Rural Healthcare

## Snake Bites
If someone is bitten by a snake, keep them calm and still. Immobilize the bitten limb and keep it below heart level. Do not cut the wound or try to suck out the venom. Seek immediate medical attention. Identify the snake if possible, but do not try to catch it.

## First Aid for Cuts and Wounds
Wash your hands before touching the wound. Clean the wound with mild soap and water. Apply an antibiotic ointment and cover it with a sterile bandage. Change the bandage daily. If the wound is deep or won't stop bleeding, see a doctor.

## Common Cold and Flu
Drink plenty of fluids and get rest. Over-the-counter pain relievers can help with fever and aches. Use a humidifier or saline nose drops for congestion. Seek medical help if you have difficulty breathing or a very high fever.

## Dehydration in Children
Symptoms include dry mouth, no tears when crying, and fewer wet diapers. Offer small, frequent sips of Oral Rehydration Solution (ORS). Avoid sugary drinks. If the child is lethargic or cannot keep fluids down, go to the hospital.

## Heat Stroke
Symptoms include high body temperature, confusion, and rapid pulse. Move the person to a cool place. Cool them down with wet cloths or a cool bath. Do not give them anything to drink if they are unconscious. Call for emergency help.
